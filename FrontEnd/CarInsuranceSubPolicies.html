Car insurance protects you and your vehicle from unexpected events. Explore our subpolicies to find the coverage that meets your needs.

Liability Coverage

Liability coverage safeguards you against financial losses if you're found responsible for causing injury or property damage to another person while driving. It's a critical component of any car insurance policy.

Key Benefits:

  • Covers bodily injuries to other drivers, passengers, or pedestrians involved in an accident.
  • Provides coverage for repairs or replacements of damaged property, such as vehicles, fences, or buildings.
  • Meets mandatory insurance requirements in most states and provinces.
  • Covers legal defense costs if you're sued for damages resulting from an accident.
  • Provides financial protection for significant, high-cost accidents.
  • Customizable limits to match your financial protection needs.

Common Scenarios:

  • Paying for medical bills and lost wages of other parties after an at-fault accident.
  • Covering the costs of repairing a neighbor's property you accidentally damaged.
  • Legal assistance and settlements in lawsuits resulting from accidents.

Why It’s Essential:

  • Protects your assets from being seized in lawsuits.
  • Ensures compliance with legal driving requirements.
  • Offers peace of mind knowing you're financially covered for unforeseen accidents.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage

This coverage protects you if you're in an accident with a driver who lacks sufficient insurance or has no insurance at all. It ensures you're not left to shoulder the financial burden of someone else's negligence.

Key Benefits:

  • Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers after an accident with an uninsured or underinsured driver.
  • Provides compensation for lost wages due to injuries sustained in the accident.
  • Offers coverage for repairs or replacement of your vehicle if damaged.
  • Protects against hit-and-run accidents, ensuring you’re not financially disadvantaged.
  • Fills the gap left by insufficient coverage from the at-fault driver.

Scenarios Covered:

  • Accidents with drivers who only carry the minimum required insurance, which doesn’t fully cover your damages.
  • Incidents involving uninsured drivers who flee the scene.
  • Compensation for pain and suffering caused by the accident.

Additional Perks:

  • Protection for pedestrians or cyclists hit by uninsured motorists.
  • Covers family members driving your insured vehicle.
  • Offers peace of mind during international travel, depending on policy terms.

Personal Insurance Protection (PIP)

Personal Insurance Protection, or PIP, goes beyond basic medical coverage by offering comprehensive support for expenses related to injuries sustained in an accident, regardless of fault.

Key Benefits:

  • Covers medical bills for injuries sustained by you, your passengers, and authorized drivers.
  • Compensates for lost income during recovery from accident-related injuries.
  • Provides financial support for essential services, such as childcare and housekeeping, if you're unable to perform them.
  • Includes coverage for rehabilitation services like physical therapy.
  • Offers death benefits to beneficiaries in the unfortunate event of a fatal accident.

Who Benefits:

  • Families with young children who rely on dual incomes.
  • Individuals seeking coverage for non-medical accident expenses.
  • Drivers who want added security for passengers in their vehicle.

Why Choose PIP:

  • Minimizes financial stress after an accident by covering non-medical expenses.
  • Ensures quick reimbursement for out-of-pocket costs.
  • Provides a safety net, even in no-fault states or provinces.

Terms and Conditions

Liability

Coverage Scope: Covers bodily injury and property damage caused to others when the policyholder is at fault in an accident.

Policyholder Obligations: Accidents must be reported to the insurer and local authorities within 24 hours.

Exclusions: Coverage does not apply to accidents involving criminal activities (e.g., DUI, reckless driving) or racing events.

Policy Limits: Coverage amounts are subject to the policy’s per-accident and per-person limits.

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist

Covered Incidents: Provides compensation for injuries or damages caused by an uninsured or underinsured driver.

Required Documentation: Claims must include evidence of the other driver’s lack of insurance and proof of damages (e.g., repair invoices, medical bills).

Policyholder Responsibilities: The policyholder must cooperate with the insurer’s investigation and legal proceedings if required.

Exclusions: Damages caused while the policyholder was engaging in illegal activities are excluded.

Personal Insurance Protection (PIP)

Scope of Coverage: Covers medical expenses, lost wages, and rehabilitation costs resulting from an accident, regardless of fault.

Claim Filing: Claims must include medical bills, proof of income, and other relevant documents.

Policy Limits: Benefits are capped as per the policy’s stated limits and may include a deductible.

Exclusions: Coverage does not apply to injuries sustained while committing illegal acts or outside the scope of normal driving activities.
