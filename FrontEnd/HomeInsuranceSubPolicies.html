Explore Our Home Insurance Subpolicies

Your home is one of your most valuable assets. Explore our subpolicies to ensure it’s protected against every eventuality.

Dwelling Coverage

Dwelling coverage protects the physical structure of your home, including the roof, walls, and foundation, against damage caused by covered events such as fire, storms, or vandalism.

Key Benefits:

  • Covers the cost of repairing or rebuilding your home after a covered peril, such as fire or windstorm.
  • Includes coverage for attached structures like garages, decks, and patios.
  • Provides protection for high-cost repairs, ensuring financial peace of mind.
  • Offers additional coverage options for unique home features like swimming pools.
  • Ensures compliance with local building codes during reconstruction.

Who Needs This:

  • Homeowners in areas prone to severe weather events or natural disasters.
  • Individuals looking to safeguard significant investments in their property.
  • Anyone seeking long-term financial protection for home-related incidents.

Personal Property Coverage

Personal property coverage protects your belongings, including furniture, electronics, clothing, and other personal items, against theft, fire, or other covered risks.

Key Benefits:

  • Compensation for lost, stolen, or damaged items such as laptops, jewelry, and appliances.
  • Covers belongings both inside and outside your home, including while traveling.
  • Offers optional riders for high-value items like antiques or artwork.
  • Replacement cost coverage ensures you receive the full value to replace items, regardless of depreciation.
  • Includes coverage for personal items stored in off-site locations, such as storage units.

Why It’s Important:

  • Protects against financial loss due to unexpected events like burglaries or fires.
  • Helps maintain your lifestyle by covering essential belongings.
  • Ensures peace of mind for travelers and those with valuable possessions.

Liability Protection

Liability protection safeguards you financially if someone is injured on your property or if you accidentally cause damage to someone else’s property.

Key Benefits:

  • Covers legal and medical costs in case of lawsuits stemming from accidents on your property.
  • Applies to incidents occurring both on and off your property, such as accidentally damaging a neighbor's fence.
  • Provides peace of mind for unforeseen accidents involving guests, delivery personnel, or contractors.
  • Includes coverage for libel, slander, or defamation lawsuits in some policies.
  • Offers high liability limits to protect your financial assets and future earnings.

Scenarios Covered:

  • Guest slips and falls on your icy driveway, requiring medical attention.
  • Your child accidentally breaks a neighbor’s window while playing sports.
  • You’re sued for damages caused by a tree from your property falling onto your neighbor’s house.

Natural Disasters Coverage

Natural disasters coverage offers additional protection for your home and belongings against major natural events like earthquakes, floods, or hurricanes.

Key Benefits:

  • Specialized coverage for disaster-prone areas, ensuring comprehensive protection.
  • Covers repair or replacement of your home and belongings damaged by natural events.
  • Provides temporary living expenses if your home is uninhabitable due to a disaster.
  • Includes debris removal and cleanup costs after an event.
  • Offers additional endorsements for specific disasters like wildfires or tsunamis.

Who It’s For:

  • Homeowners in regions with frequent natural disasters, such as coastal or earthquake-prone areas.
  • Those seeking added security beyond standard homeowner policies.
  • Families prioritizing quick recovery and rebuilding after a disaster strikes.

Terms and Conditions

Dwelling

Scope of Coverage: Covers physical damages to the insured home caused by fire, wind, hail, vandalism, or other perils specified in the policy.

Exclusions: Damages caused by lack of maintenance, intentional destruction, or pre-existing structural issues are not covered.

Filing a Claim: Notify the insurer within 30 days of an incident, provide photographs of the damage, and cooperate with inspections.

Repair/Replacement Costs: Applies to repair or replacement costs up to the policy limit, subject to a deductible.

Personal Property

Covered Items: Includes furniture, electronics, clothing, and other belongings against theft, fire, and specified perils.

High-Value Items: Jewelry, art, and valuables must be declared and insured under separate riders for full protection.

Proof of Ownership: Claims require receipts, photographs, or documentation to verify ownership and value.

Exclusions: Losses from negligence, wear and tear, or non-covered events (e.g., flooding) are excluded.

Liability Protection

Scope of Coverage: Covers legal liabilities for injuries or property damage caused to others on the insured property.

Policy Holder Obligations: Notify the insurer of incidents that may lead to claims and avoid admitting fault.

Exclusions: Intentional harm, criminal activities, or liabilities from business activities on the property are not covered.

Coverage Limits: Payments for damages or legal defense are capped at the policy’s liability limit.

Natural Disasters

Covered Events: Includes damages caused by storms, hurricanes, wildfires, and specified natural disasters.

Flood and Earthquake Riders: Standard policies exclude floods and earthquakes unless riders are purchased.

Filing a Claim: Submit evidence such as photographs, repair estimates, and an incident report.

Exclusions: Damages resulting from neglect, such as failing to secure windows during a storm, are excluded.
