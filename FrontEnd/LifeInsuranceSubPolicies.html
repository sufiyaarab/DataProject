Explore Our Life Insurance Policies

Life insurance provides the financial security your loved ones deserve. Explore the subpolicies to find a plan that fits your needs and goals.

Term Life Insurance

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period, ensuring financial stability for your loved ones during critical years.

Key Features:

  • Lower premiums compared to permanent life insurance.
  • Coverage terms ranging from 10 to 30 years to suit your needs.
  • Option to convert to permanent life insurance as circumstances change.

Who It’s For:

  • Young families looking to secure financial support for children.
  • Homeowners wanting to ensure mortgage repayment in case of death.
  • Individuals with temporary financial responsibilities, such as student loans.

Additional Benefits:

  • Flexibility to customize coverage amounts for specific financial goals.
  • Peace of mind knowing your family is protected during crucial years.

Whole Life Insurance

Whole life insurance provides permanent coverage with added benefits of cash value accumulation, making it a reliable choice for long-term financial planning.

Key Features:

  • Lifelong coverage with guaranteed payouts to beneficiaries.
  • Tax-deferred cash value growth that increases over time.
  • Fixed premiums that remain constant throughout the policy.

Why Choose Whole Life Insurance:

  • Builds a financial safety net for your family’s future.
  • Helps fund significant life events, such as college tuition or weddings.
  • Ideal for estate planning to leave a legacy for heirs.

Additional Uses:

  • Cash value can be borrowed against to address emergencies or investments.
  • Provides financial support for charitable giving upon death.
  • Acts as a savings tool for individuals with long-term goals.

Universal Life Insurance

Universal life insurance offers lifelong coverage with the flexibility to adjust premiums and benefits, ensuring your policy evolves with your financial goals.

Key Features:

  • Adjustable premiums to suit your financial situation over time.
  • Builds cash value with interest, offering financial flexibility.
  • Flexible death benefits to adapt to changing family needs.

Who It’s For:

  • Individuals with fluctuating incomes who need premium flexibility.
  • Families seeking a combination of life insurance and savings.
  • Those planning for significant expenses like education or retirement.

Additional Benefits:

  • Tax-deferred cash value growth to supplement retirement income.
  • Access to funds for emergencies through policy loans or withdrawals.
  • Ability to increase coverage as financial responsibilities grow.

Final Expense Insurance

Final expense insurance provides financial support for end-of-life costs, helping ease the burden on your loved ones. It ensures funds are available for funeral expenses, medical bills, and small outstanding debts.

What It Covers:

  • Funeral and burial costs, including memorial services.
  • Outstanding medical expenses.
  • Personal debts or small financial obligations.

Key Benefits:

  • No medical exams for most policies, ensuring easy approval.
  • Quick payout to beneficiaries for immediate needs.
  • Affordable premiums with coverage ranging from $5,000 to $25,000.

Who It’s For:

  • Individuals seeking a straightforward plan for end-of-life costs.
  • Those without substantial savings for final expenses.
  • Anyone wanting to minimize financial stress on their family.

Terms and Conditions

Term Life

Policy Duration: Coverage applies for the specified term (e.g., 10, 20, or 30 years).

Exclusions: Suicide within the first two years is excluded from coverage.

Policyholder Obligations: Premiums must be paid on time to maintain coverage.

Claims: Beneficiaries must provide a death certificate and other documentation to file a claim.

Whole Life

Lifelong Coverage: Policy remains in effect as long as premiums are paid.

Cash Value: Accumulated cash value can be borrowed against or withdrawn, subject to terms.

Exclusions: Policies do not cover death due to illegal activities or suicide within the contestability period.

Premiums: Premiums remain fixed for the duration of the policy.

Universal Life

Flexible Premiums: Policyholders can adjust premium amounts and death benefits.

Interest Rates: Cash value grows based on interest rates, which are subject to change.

Minimum Balance: A minimum account balance must be maintained to keep the policy active.

Withdrawals: Partial withdrawals reduce the death benefit and may incur charges.

Final Expense

Purpose: Coverage is intended for funeral expenses, medical bills, and other end-of-life costs.

Limited Coverage: Coverage amounts are lower than traditional life insurance policies.

Claims: Beneficiaries must provide itemized receipts and proof of expenses.

Eligibility: Policies may have simplified underwriting with fewer medical exams.
